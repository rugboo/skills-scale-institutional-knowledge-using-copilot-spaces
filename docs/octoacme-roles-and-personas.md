# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UI/UX Designer

### Role Summary
UI/UX Designers create intuitive, accessible user interfaces and experiences. They collaborate with Product Managers, Project Managers, and Developers to ensure designs meet user needs and align with business goals.

### Responsibilities
- Design interfaces, wireframes, and prototypes
- Conduct usability testing and user research
- Maintain design systems and style guides
- Collaborate with Developers to ensure design fidelity
- Incorporate user feedback into iterative design improvements

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered designs that improve usability
- Ensure consistency across product interfaces
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product and Development teams
- Usability testing feedback sessions
- Design handoff documentation for Developers

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create, maintain, and improve documentation for processes, products, and onboarding. They ensure information is clear, consistent, and accessible across teams.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain process documentation and user guides
- Support onboarding by creating training materials
- Align documentation with product changes and releases
- Collaborate with Developers, Product, and Support to gather accurate information
- Review and edit content for clarity and consistency

### Goals
- Ensure documentation is accurate, up-to-date, and accessible
- Reduce knowledge gaps and onboarding time
- Support cross-team alignment through clear written communication

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews with stakeholders
- Release note coordination with Product and Development
- Onboarding feedback sessions with new team members

---

## Support Lead

### Role Summary
Support Leads bridge users and engineering by escalating feedback, coordinating on incidents, and advocating for customer needs. They ensure user issues are addressed and learnings feed back into product improvements.

### Responsibilities
- Aggregate and escalate user feedback to Product and Engineering
- Coordinate incident response and communication with users
- Maintain support documentation and FAQ resources
- Identify patterns in user issues and recommend improvements
- Collaborate with QA and Developers on bug triage

### Goals
- Improve customer satisfaction and support efficiency
- Reduce incident resolution time
- Ensure user feedback influences product priorities

### Typical Communication
- Incident updates and escalation to Engineering
- User feedback summaries for Product and Project Managers
- Coordination with Technical Writers on support documentation

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers lead CI/CD, automation, and deployment reliability. They collaborate with Developers and QA to ensure code flows smoothly from development to production with minimal friction and risk.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Automate testing, security scanning, and deployment processes
- Monitor infrastructure and deployment health
- Support rollback and incident recovery procedures
- Collaborate with Developers and QA on deployment best practices

### Goals
- Ensure reliable, fast, and secure deployments
- Reduce manual intervention and deployment risk
- Improve observability and incident response

### Typical Communication
- Deployment planning and post-mortems with Development and QA
- Pipeline status updates and release coordination
- Infrastructure and tooling updates for the team

## Role Onboarding & Handoff Checklist

Use this checklist when onboarding a new team member to a role or handing off responsibilities between roles.

### Onboarding Checklist
- [ ] Introduce role summary, responsibilities, and goals
- [ ] Share relevant documentation and process guides
- [ ] Schedule introductions with key collaborators (PM, PdM, Dev, QA, etc.)
- [ ] Provide access to project boards, repos, and communication channels
- [ ] Review current project status, risks, and upcoming milestones
- [ ] Assign a mentor or buddy for the first few weeks

### Handoff Checklist
- [ ] Document current work status and outstanding items
- [ ] Update project boards and risk registers with latest information
- [ ] Brief incoming team member on context, dependencies, and stakeholders
- [ ] Transfer ownership of artifacts, docs, and communication threads
- [ ] Schedule a follow-up check-in after handoff is complete
